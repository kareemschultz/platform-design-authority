# Packaging and Platform Editions

## Purpose

Define an understandable commercial structure that supports small businesses, growing multi-location organizations, enterprises, and partners without exposing customers to the platform's full internal complexity.

## Recommended Editions

### Starter

For small organizations needing one principal workflow with guided setup, standard support, limited locations, and sensible usage allowances.

### Growth

For expanding organizations needing multiple locations, automation, stronger reporting, additional roles, and selected integrations.

### Professional

For complex operations needing advanced approvals, configurable workflows, APIs, deeper analytics, offline capabilities, and multiple domain bundles.

### Enterprise

For large or regulated organizations needing SSO, SCIM, advanced security, delegated administration, data residency, dedicated environments, stronger service levels, and enterprise implementation.

### Platform Partner

For resellers, managed-service providers, franchisors, and software partners managing branded customer portfolios, delegated administration, catalogs, billing relationships, and revenue share.

## Packaging Rules

1. Editions establish defaults and commercial simplicity; they do not create separate applications.
2. Customers may add domain bundles, capabilities, usage, industry packs, and services within compatibility rules.
3. Capability identifiers remain stable even when bundles and edition names change.
4. Published offers must show included limits and overage behavior.
5. Enterprise-only restrictions require a cost, risk, service, or governance rationale—not artificial inconvenience.
6. Grandfathering, migrations, and price changes require versioned offer records.

## Domain Bundle Examples

- Commerce and Retail
- Inventory and Warehouse
- Procurement and Supply Chain
- Finance
- CRM and Marketing
- Workforce and Payroll
- Manufacturing and Maintenance
- Projects and Professional Services
- Service and Field Operations
- Governance, Documents, and Analytics

## Add-on Examples

- Additional locations, registers, warehouses, legal entities, or workers
- Advanced AI and automation
- White-label branding and custom domains
- Enterprise SSO and SCIM
- Offline and edge operations
- Advanced reporting and data feeds
- Premium integrations
- Dedicated environment or regional hosting

## Anti-Complexity Rule

The public catalog should present a small number of clear starting offers. Fine-grained capability composition remains available behind guided configuration, sales-assisted quoting, and partner catalogs.
