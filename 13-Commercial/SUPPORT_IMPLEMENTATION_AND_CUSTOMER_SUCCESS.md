# Support, Implementation, and Customer Success

## Purpose

Define commercial services that help customers deploy, adopt, operate, expand, and receive support without making successful use dependent on expensive consulting.

## Support Tiers

### Standard

Knowledge base, in-product guidance, community or ticket support, standard response targets, and status communications.

### Professional

Faster response targets, named support context, implementation guidance, integration support, and scheduled health reviews.

### Enterprise

Defined service levels, escalation management, security and architecture support, release coordination, incident communication, and optional dedicated technical account management.

### Partner Managed

Partner provides first-line support under platform standards, with governed escalation and customer visibility.

## Implementation Packages

- Guided self-service
- Quick start
- Standard implementation
- Advanced multi-location or multi-domain implementation
- Enterprise transformation
- Data migration
- Integration and extension services
- White-label and partner launch

## Rules

1. Product onboarding must make simple customers successful without mandatory consulting.
2. Implementation scope, assumptions, deliverables, responsibilities, acceptance, and change control must be explicit.
3. Data migration includes profiling, mapping, cleansing, rehearsal, reconciliation, cutover, rollback, and archival.
4. Customer-specific code is a last resort and must use approved extension points.
5. Support access is time-limited, permissioned, visible, and audited.
6. Service levels distinguish response, restoration, resolution, and workaround commitments.
7. Customer success measures adoption and outcomes, not login counts alone.

## Customer Success Lifecycle

Discover, onboard, configure, migrate, launch, stabilize, adopt, optimize, expand, renew, and advocate.

## Health Signals

- Workflow completion and time-to-value
- Active role and workspace adoption
- Configuration and data quality
- Integration and job failures
- Support volume and sentiment
- Usage against purchased capacity
- Critical capability adoption
- Renewal, expansion, and churn risk

## Exit and Transition

Customers must receive documented data export, knowledge transfer, integration shutdown, credential revocation, retention, and transition support according to contract.
