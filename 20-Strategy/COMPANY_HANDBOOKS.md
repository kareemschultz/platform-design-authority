# Company Handbooks

## Purpose

Define the operating handbooks required to build, sell, implement, support, and govern the platform company consistently.

## Product Handbook

Covers discovery, problem selection, capability proposals, roadmap, prioritization, outcome metrics, experiments, releases, deprecation, and customer feedback.

## Design Handbook

Covers research, information architecture, interaction design, design system, content, accessibility, usability testing, prototypes, handoff, and experience measurement.

## Engineering Handbook

Covers architecture, code, review, testing, security, release, operations, incidents, documentation, and technical career expectations.

## AI Handbook

Covers acceptable use, prompt and agent development, evaluations, data policy, approval levels, incidents, provider review, and customer communication.

## Implementation Handbook

Covers discovery, Business DNA, configuration, migration, integrations, training, cutover, stabilization, acceptance, and transition to support.

## Support Handbook

Covers intake, severity, response, troubleshooting, support access, escalation, incidents, customer communication, knowledge, and problem management.

## Customer Success Handbook

Covers onboarding, adoption, health, outcomes, risk, optimization, renewal, expansion, and advocacy.

## Partner Handbook

Covers application, due diligence, certification, delegated administration, implementation standards, support, branding, revenue, audit, and exit.

## Sales Handbook

Covers qualification, discovery, demonstrations, solution fit, security and legal claims, pricing, proposals, contracting, handoff, and prohibited promises.

## Governance

Each handbook identifies owner, audience, decision rights, mandatory processes, metrics, templates, and escalation. Handbooks cannot contradict the Constitution, ADRs, approved specifications, contracts, or law.

## Initial Priority

1. Product
2. Design
3. Engineering
4. Implementation
5. Support
6. AI
7. Customer Success
8. Partner
9. Sales

Detailed role procedures are written before the corresponding operating function scales beyond the founding team.
