# Customer Success Handbook

## Purpose

Define how customers achieve measurable outcomes after implementation through adoption, health management, optimization, renewal, and expansion.

## Lifecycle

Onboard, launch, stabilize, adopt, optimize, expand, renew, advocate, and transition or exit.

## Success Plan

Each customer has agreed outcomes, workflows, baseline measures, target measures, owners, milestones, risks, review cadence, and support model.

## Health

Health combines:

- Workflow adoption
- Time to value
- Data and configuration quality
- Integration and job health
- Support burden
- User and role coverage
- Usage against capacity
- Executive engagement
- Outcome progress
- Commercial and renewal risk

Health is explainable and never based on hidden sensitive profiling.

## Reviews

Operational reviews address workflow performance and issues. Executive reviews address outcomes, risk, roadmap, value, and next priorities.

## Expansion

Recommend expansion only when the current capability is stable and the added scope solves a verified problem. Entitlement recommendations do not bypass customer approval.

## Renewal

Renewal evidence includes achieved outcomes, adoption, unresolved risks, support history, roadmap fit, price and usage transparency, and implementation burden.

## Churn and Exit

Capture reasons honestly, preserve export and transition rights, revoke access safely, and feed product and implementation learning back into the company.

## Metrics

Time to value, adoption by critical role, outcome attainment, health accuracy, renewal, expansion, churn reason, support effort, and advocacy.
