# Partner Handbook

## Purpose

Define how referral, reseller, implementation, managed-service, technology, marketplace, franchise, and strategic partners are selected, enabled, governed, supported, and exited.

## Lifecycle

Application, due diligence, agreement, training, certification, sandbox, active delivery, quality monitoring, remediation, suspension, termination, and archival.

## Partner Authority

All partner access is delegated, scoped, revocable, visible, and audited. A partner cannot create hidden administrators, bypass tenant consent, weaken minimum security, or expand entitlements outside contract.

## Certification

Certification evaluates product knowledge, implementation, migration, security, privacy, support, commercial conduct, and industry expertise. Credentials expire and require continuing evidence.

## Customer Relationship

The customer must understand contracting, billing, support, data-controller, implementation, and escalation responsibilities.

## Delivery Quality

Partners follow the Implementation and Support handbooks, approved extensions, migration controls, documentation, acceptance, and handoff requirements.

## Branding

White-label and co-brand rights follow contractual tiers. Branding cannot obscure the responsible legal and support parties or weaken security indicators.

## Commercial Governance

Referral, margin, revenue share, marketplace, services, credits, refunds, disputes, taxes, and clawbacks are versioned and reconcilable.

## Monitoring

Track implementation outcomes, support quality, customer satisfaction, security incidents, audit findings, certification, revenue, churn, and policy violations.

## Exit

Define customer transfer, data and credential handoff, domain and application ownership, support continuity, commission settlement, and revocation.
