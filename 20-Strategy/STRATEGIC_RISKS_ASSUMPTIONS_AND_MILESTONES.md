# Strategic Risks, Assumptions, and Milestones

## Purpose

Define the assumptions that must prove true, the risks that could invalidate the strategy, and the milestones used to make evidence-based continuation decisions.

## Critical Assumptions

- Guyana retailers have painful unmet workflow needs.
- Customers will pay for a unified modular platform.
- Offline reliability and regional payments create differentiation.
- A TypeScript modular monolith can support the first growth stages.
- The company can support statutory and payment obligations responsibly.
- Implementation can be productized rather than dominated by custom services.
- Partners can expand reach without degrading quality.

## Strategic Risks

- Scope expansion before product-market evidence
- Regulatory or payment-model error
- Weak cash and offline execution
- Underestimated implementation and support burden
- Platform complexity harming usability
- Provider or cloud dependency
- Security or tenant-isolation failure
- Founder bandwidth and financing constraints
- Inadequate local legal, tax, and accounting support
- Competitors improving faster than expected

## Milestones

1. Blueprint audit and founder decisions
2. Controlled technical prototypes
3. Named design partners
4. First-slice implementation-ready specifications
5. Pilot launch
6. Measured workflow superiority
7. Repeatable implementation
8. Paid production customers
9. Partner-assisted delivery
10. Adjacent capability expansion

## Decision Reviews

At each milestone review evidence, cost, risk, customer outcomes, team capacity, and opportunity cost. Continue, narrow, pause, partner, or stop explicitly.

## Risk Record

Each risk records owner, likelihood, impact, leading indicators, mitigation, contingency, trigger, and review date.
