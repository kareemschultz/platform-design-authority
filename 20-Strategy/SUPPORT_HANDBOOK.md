# Support Handbook

## Purpose

Define support intake, severity, diagnostics, access, escalation, customer communication, problem management, and knowledge creation.

## Company-Stage Note

This is a founding-stage operating design. It defines the support capability required before pilot and production; it does not imply a staffed round-the-clock support organization or contracted response commitments. Operational specifications and signed customer terms are authoritative for actual service levels.

## Intake

Capture customer, tenant, environment, affected workflow, business impact, time, scope, recent changes, evidence, and safe reproduction details.

## Severity

Severity reflects business impact, security, privacy, financial, statutory, and workaround considerations—not customer tone alone.

## Support Access

Access is named, approved, scoped, time-limited, step-up authenticated, visible, and audited. Support does not request passwords, factors, or raw secrets.

## Troubleshooting

Use diagnostics, traces, safe projections, runbooks, and reproducible steps before direct data access. Preserve tenant isolation and classification.

## Escalation

Escalate to Engineering, Security, Privacy, Finance, Provider Operations, or Incident Command with clear ownership and next update time.

## Communication

State impact, current understanding, workaround, next action, next update, and resolution. Do not claim final resolution before reconciliation.

## Problem Management

Recurring incidents create a problem record, root-cause analysis, corrective actions, owner, deadline, regression test, documentation update, and customer follow-up.

## Knowledge

Validated resolutions become searchable articles with audience, scope, version, symptoms, cause, safe steps, and escalation criteria.

## Metrics

- First response
- Time to restoration and resolution
- Reopen rate
- Escalation rate
- Customer effort
- Recurring issue rate
- Knowledge reuse
- Support-access risk events
