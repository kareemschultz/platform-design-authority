# Meridian Differentiation Manifest

## Purpose and claim boundary

This manifest synthesizes competitive research completed through 2026-07-16. It names product hypotheses and design obligations, not proven competitive advantages. Constitution, ADRs, specifications, registries, founder decisions, first-slice scope, and implementation evidence remain authoritative.

## Table stakes

- Fast, accessible search and task navigation with visible context.
- Permission-aware administration, approvals, audit, import/export, notifications, mobile and recovery.
- Explicit lifecycle workflows for finance, inventory, sales, service, workforce, and provider operations.
- Partial, exceptional, corrected, offline/degraded, and reconciled states—not only happy paths.
- Governed metrics, source-linked drill-down, documentation, release communication, and support handoff.

## Durable differentiation candidates

| Candidate | Why it may matter | Current evidence state |
|---|---|---|
| authority-visible workflows | suites frequently blur projection, source, and owner | Supported research; Prototype Required |
| one correction grammar | reversal/compensation, effective date, reason, diff and audit across consequential domains | Supported research; Prototype Required |
| honest uncertainty | queued, pending, unknown, stale, conflicted and needs-review states across providers/offline | Supported research; Prototype Required |
| shared review mechanics with domain-owned decisions | reduces scattered exceptions without centralizing truth | Supported research; Prototype Required |
| capability evidence lifecycle | separates proposed, configured, tested, certified and commercially available | Supported research; governance prototype exists, market value unproven |
| deterministic AI-disabled operation | preserves essential work under cost, refusal, outage or policy disablement | Strong authority fit; implementation evidence absent |
| governed regional packs | global contracts plus jurisdiction evidence gates | Strategic hypothesis; founder/legal/provider evidence required |

## Intentional exclusions

Broad autonomous AI, payment facilitation/custody, paid marketplace billing/payout, arbitrary third-party core-process code, global payroll claims, production fiscal submission, full GL/financial statements, production storefront/recurring commerce, self-checkout, and unverified terminal coverage remain excluded or deferred by current authority.

## Things Meridian must never copy

Module-count positioning, ERP-maze navigation, shared mutable master data without ownership, destructive financial/inventory/payroll correction, “offline” without limits and certainty, provider SDKs as business contracts, prompt-based authorization, contact-equals-Party, inaccessible drag/hover-only workflows, and marketing availability presented as implementation readiness.

## Required proof before claims

Each candidate needs linked prototype tests, accessibility evidence, tenant-isolation/security evidence, usability evidence for the target role, performance/recovery exercises, operational ownership, customer/pilot validation, and precise claim language. Research alone cannot advance EVID entries.

## Confidence, contradictions, and revalidation

Confidence is high that the control problems recur across product categories; confidence is low that Meridian's response will outperform alternatives before implementation and pilot evidence. Revalidate quarterly, at scope admission, and when a major vendor/product category changes materially.
