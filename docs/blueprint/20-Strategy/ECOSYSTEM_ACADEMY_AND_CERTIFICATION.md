# Ecosystem, Academy, and Certification

## Purpose

Define how the platform develops a trustworthy ecosystem of customers, administrators, implementers, developers, support professionals, partners, and marketplace publishers.

## Academy Tracks

- End user
- Tenant administrator
- Finance and accounting operator
- Inventory and warehouse operator
- Workforce and payroll operator
- Implementation consultant
- Developer and integration engineer
- Marketplace publisher
- Support specialist
- Partner sales and solution consultant

## Learning Model

Use role-based learning paths, realistic scenarios, labs, assessments, reference workflows, and continuing education. Training should measure successful work, not video completion.

## Certification

Certifications define prerequisites, assessed capabilities, practical exercises, passing standards, validity period, renewal, ethics, and revocation.

## Partner Tiers

Partner status may consider certified staff, implementation outcomes, support quality, customer satisfaction, security, marketplace quality, and regional expertise—not revenue alone.

## Content Governance

Training content is versioned with platform releases. Deprecated workflows are removed or clearly marked. Jurisdiction-specific courses require current evidence and reviewers.

## Community

Community programs may include forums, events, office hours, examples, open-source tools, and feedback channels while protecting customer data and avoiding unsupported advice.

## Commercial Model

Academy content may be free, included with subscriptions, sold as courses, bundled with partner programs, or required for certification. Pricing must not create artificial barriers to safe product use.

## Quality Measures

Task success after training, implementation quality, certification reliability, support reduction, learner satisfaction, renewal, partner outcomes, and customer business impact.
