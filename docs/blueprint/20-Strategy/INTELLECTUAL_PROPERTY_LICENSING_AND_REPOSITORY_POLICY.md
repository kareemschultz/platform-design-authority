# Intellectual Property, Licensing, and Repository Policy

## Purpose

Define ownership, licensing, contribution, disclosure, open-source use, public-repository boundaries, and protection of customer and company assets.

## Asset Classes

- Platform source code
- Architecture and documentation
- Trademarks and brand assets
- Customer configuration and data
- Extensions and marketplace packages
- Training and academy content
- Research, benchmarks, and datasets
- AI prompts, evaluations, and generated artifacts

## Ownership

Contracts must identify the entity owning platform IP, employee and contractor assignment, customer ownership, partner rights, and extension publisher rights.

## Repository Visibility

Public content may support trust, recruiting, ecosystem growth, and thought leadership. Private content protects security details, commercial strategy, customer information, unreleased capabilities, and operational procedures.

A visibility review classifies each repository or path before publication.

## Open Source

Dependencies and released components require license review, attribution, compatibility, security, maintenance ownership, and a contribution policy.

## Contributions

External contributions require contributor terms, code review, security review, tests, provenance, and documentation. Contribution does not automatically confer product direction or marketplace approval.

## Customer and Partner Content

Customer data and configuration remain governed by contract and privacy policy. White-label assets and partner content are isolated and exportable according to agreement.

## AI and Data

Training or evaluation use of customer data requires explicit approved basis. Generated content does not remove source-license, confidentiality, or privacy obligations.

## Required Decisions

- Platform legal entity
- Public versus private blueprint policy
- Documentation license
- Source-code licensing model
- Contributor agreement
- Marketplace publisher terms
- Trademark strategy

Qualified legal review is required before ratification.
