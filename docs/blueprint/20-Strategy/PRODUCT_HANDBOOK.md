# Product Handbook

## Purpose

Define how product opportunities move from evidence to approved capabilities and measured outcomes.

## Product Process

1. Observe customer work and business outcomes.
2. Define problem, persona, context, frequency, severity, and willingness to pay.
3. Map existing capabilities and ownership.
4. Research competitors and alternatives.
5. Propose outcome, scope, exclusions, risks, and measures.
6. Create or update capability and blueprint specifications.
7. Obtain architecture, security, UX, commercial, and jurisdiction review.
8. Prototype the highest-risk assumptions.
9. Deliver a narrow vertical workflow.
10. Measure, improve, expand, or retire.

## Prioritization

Evaluate customer value, strategic fit, reuse, urgency, evidence, implementation cost, operational burden, regulatory risk, and opportunity cost. Feature count is not a strategy.

## Product Requirements

A capability proposal includes user problem, workflow, data owner, permissions, entitlements, states, APIs, events, mobile, offline, accessibility, analytics, AI, operations, migration, support, and success metrics.

## Discovery

Use interviews, observation, support cases, implementation feedback, telemetry, win/loss analysis, and competitor evidence. Do not treat one loud request as a market.

## Roadmap

Roadmaps communicate problems, outcomes, dependencies, confidence, and sequencing—not guaranteed dates for unvalidated work.

## Release

Each release has target users, rollout, documentation, migration, support, telemetry, rollback, and post-release review.

## Deprecation

Measure use, notify affected customers, provide migration, preserve data rights, and avoid permanent product clutter.
