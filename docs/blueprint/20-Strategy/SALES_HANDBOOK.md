# Sales Handbook

## Purpose

Define qualification, discovery, demonstration, solution fit, pricing, proposals, security and legal claims, contracting, and delivery handoff.

## Company-Stage Note

This is a founding-stage operating design. It describes the process the company must establish as sales activity begins; it does not imply that a staffed sales organization, published pricing, certifications, customer references, or production capabilities already exist.

Approved specifications under `13-Commercial/` are authoritative for editions, entitlements, billing, partners, services, marketplace, and contractual money flows. This handbook summarizes operating behavior and cannot override them.

## Qualification

Assess business problem, current systems, workflows, locations, users, data, integrations, jurisdiction, timeline, budget, decision process, implementation capacity, and measurable outcome.

## Discovery

Use Business DNA and workflow discovery. Understand exceptions, offline needs, security, payments, tax, reporting, migration, support, and success criteria.

## Demonstrations

Demonstrate the customer's workflow using truthful, supported capabilities. Distinguish current product, prototype, planned capability, partner solution, and custom work.

## Prohibited Promises

Sales may not promise:

- Unapproved roadmap dates
- Statutory or regulatory compliance without verified scope
- Provider availability not confirmed for the customer's entity and region
- Unlimited customization
- AI accuracy or autonomy beyond evidence
- Payment-facilitator or merchant-of-record services
- Security certification not held
- Zero downtime, perfect migration, or unsupported deployment

## Solution and Pricing

Proposals identify edition, capabilities, limits, usage, services, deployment, support, assumptions, exclusions, responsibilities, data migration, integrations, and commercial term.

## Security and Legal Review

Use approved questionnaires and evidence. Escalate novel contract, privacy, residency, payment, tax, IP, or security commitments.

## Handoff

The signed solution transfers to Implementation with discovery evidence, scope, assumptions, decision log, success measures, risks, and customer responsibilities.

## Metrics

Qualified conversion, sales cycle, win/loss reason, discount quality, implementation variance, time to value, renewal quality, and prohibited-promise incidents.
