## Why this program exists

A nine-agent discovery sweep plus direct primary-source verification (npm registry, postgresql.org, Microsoft's pg_durable repository, shadcn/Base UI/React Aria/Studio official documentation) audited every open decision surface: all 28 ADRs, the ratification-wave machinery, the Founder Decision Register, the Architecture Risk Register, the Draft document population, every decision matrix, and the competitive-intelligence corpus.

**Headline: this is a promotion-mechanism problem plus an enforcement-gap problem, not a content problem.** Per `registry/documents.json`, 445 of 476 governed documents are Draft, 28 Proposed (all ADRs), 3 Superseded — zero Approved/Accepted/Ratified — because the ratification process (PDA-RDM-010) has never executed once. RW-00 sits in `preparation` with four recorded blockers: no independent reviewer assigned, candidate revision and version manifest not frozen, DCA-006 independent review open, and the final governance audit open. A 20-document stratified sample found no placeholder-quality drafts (deliberate, scoped placeholders only); per-document completeness claims remain subject to wave review — no broader quality claim is made here. Separately: twelve of 28 Proposed ADRs are cited in CLAUDE.md as settled law, seven without naming the ADR; three cross-document contradictions live in the 09-UX component-governance cluster; the acquisition policy contradicts both the founder's stated Studio-primary direction and the `component-intake` skill; and confirmed smaller gaps include `registry/ratification-waves.json` still recording "FDR-004 is open" three days after FDR-004's ratification, an undocumented `MAX_MCP_OUTPUT_TOKENS` operational requirement, and an ADR-0018 version-pin control with no executable anchor.

Intended outcome: every decidable decision decided and recorded at its honest depth, affected documents updated to current-lifecycle truth, enforcement automated instead of prose-only, founder-only items batched and prepared, and the ratification machine started through its own rules rather than around them.

- [ ] **G1 — Reviewer mechanism, processed as Class A via new ADR-0029, full path, no shortcut.** Agents draft **ADR-0029 "Independent Review Roles and AI-Assisted Review Boundaries"** (next free number; `docs/templates/ADR_TEMPLATE.md` format) proposing: a different-vendor AI (not the authoring model) may supply the **independent-review lens** for technical waves; all named approval authorities remain human (the founder genuinely holds the founder role); qualified specialist roles (security, accessibility, legal, penetration testing) are **not** satisfiable by AI or by founder countersignature — a wave either engages the real specialist or explicitly defers those documents per PDA-RDM-010's role-combination clause, which requires the approval authority to accept any combination with credible independence; AI eligibility, conflict declaration, provenance, and limitations are defined in the ADR. **RW-01 (Constitution) retains genuine independent human review as a hard requirement.** The framework gap from D5 (ratification decisions and ADR authorship classified by impact) is addressed inside this ADR.

  ADR-0029 is Class A per `DECISION_FRAMEWORK.md` and completes the **full** Class A path before any wave uses it — none of these steps is skippable or satisfied by the drafting agent alone:
  1. **Architecture review** — platform-architecture lens on ADR-0029's design (does the mechanism actually preserve modular-monolith/independence invariants).
  2. **Security review** — a **qualified human security reviewer**, per G1's own rule that specialist security review is not satisfiable by AI or founder countersignature; this ADR is exactly the case that rule exists for, since it defines the AI-eligibility boundary itself. An AI security lens (subagent or Codex) may run first as a **supplementary** pass — surfacing candidate gaming vectors (prompt injection, model collusion, scope creep) for the human reviewer to weigh — but does not satisfy step 2 on its own; absent an available qualified human reviewer, step 2 is **explicitly deferred** per PDA-RDM-010's role-combination clause, and ADR-0029 cannot reach Accepted while it is deferred.
  3. **Impact analysis** — enumerate every wave/document class the mechanism will govern and what independence guarantee each gets.
  4. **Independently authorized review** — the different-vendor AI review (Codex) that round 1/round 2 of this very PR's own review already demonstrates in practice; the ADR **cites this program's own review history as its worked precedent**, not as a substitute for reviewing the ADR itself.
  5. **Founder approval** — the founder's R4 decision, informed by 1–4, not standing in for them.
  6. **Disposition** — findings from 1–4 recorded against the ADR text (concur/changes/object), same discipline as any other governed decision.
  7. **Ratified/adopted lifecycle** — ADR-0029 is marked Accepted only after 1–6 are complete; it is **not usable to justify RW-00's own reviewer mechanism until it is itself accepted** (adoption is founder step 5, not drafting).
  Steps 1 and 4 are agent-executable (subagent or Codex review) and happen in R3 before R4; step 2 requires the qualified human security reviewer described above (an agent-run supplementary lens does not close it); step 3 is authored alongside the draft; steps 5–7 are the R4 founder sitting. This sequence is what prevents circularity: ADR-0029 cannot bootstrap its own authority by being the thing that approves its own review, and it cannot bootstrap its own security review by having an AI mark itself trustworthy.
