# Fable 5 Review Brief — Blueprint Foundation Through Commercial Architecture

## Review Objective

Perform an independent gap, contradiction, feasibility, and competitive-quality review of the Platform Design Authority repository before the blueprint advances into detailed AI, developer-platform, UX, data, security, deployment, testing, and implementation specifications.

## Review Scope

Review all current documents, with particular attention to:

- Foundation and constitutional coherence
- Platform kernel completeness
- Domain ownership and dependency boundaries
- Shared-engine responsibilities
- Business capability coverage
- Industry-pack gaps
- Recommended technology stack
- Better Auth integration and separation from authorization
- Next.js, TanStack, Expo, and Better-T-Stack decisions
- Commercial packaging, entitlements, metering, billing, white label, and partners

## Required Review Lenses

### Architecture

- Are domain boundaries coherent and implementable?
- Are any shared engines missing or incorrectly scoped?
- Where could the modular monolith become tightly coupled?
- Are offline, event, workflow, and data-ownership rules sufficient?

### Product and UX

- Can the platform remain intuitive despite its breadth?
- Are role-based workspaces and progressive disclosure strong enough?
- Which common workflows, personas, or customer types are absent?
- Are any features present only as labels without complete lifecycle thinking?

### Commercial

- Can plans, add-ons, trials, usage, white label, partners, and marketplace billing coexist cleanly?
- What downgrade, suspension, data, and revenue-recognition edge cases are missing?
- Is the proposed packaging understandable and sellable?

### Security and Identity

- Does Better Auth integrate safely with tenant context, custom domains, passkeys, 2FA, SSO, SCIM, mobile, and offline operation?
- Are authentication, authorization, entitlements, and business-party records sufficiently separated?
- Which threat models or enterprise identity requirements are missing?

### Technology

- Is the TypeScript, PostgreSQL, Next.js, TanStack, Expo, NestJS/Fastify, Temporal, Redis, and NATS direction coherent?
- Which decisions require benchmarks or prototypes before ratification?
- Are maturity, licensing, self-hosting, and migration risks represented honestly?

### Competitive Quality

- Which direct competitors or best-in-class specialists reveal major capability gaps?
- Which areas risk being merely feature-complete rather than meaningfully better?
- What measurable usability, performance, mobile, offline, AI, accessibility, and implementation benchmarks should be added?

## Expected Output

Return:

1. Executive assessment
2. Blocking gaps
3. Major gaps
4. Contradictions and ambiguous ownership
5. Missing capabilities, domains, engines, personas, and workflows
6. Technology risks and required experiments
7. Commercial risks and missing lifecycle cases
8. Security and compliance gaps
9. Competitive differentiators worth strengthening
10. Prioritized recommendations: Now, Before Implementation, Later
11. Suggested new or revised document names
12. Overall readiness score by repository section

## Review Rules

- Cite exact file paths and headings.
- Separate facts, interpretations, and recommendations.
- Do not recommend adding features solely to increase checklist size.
- Prefer workflow superiority, simplicity, trust, and maintainability.
- Identify where the blueprint is too broad for the first release.
- Challenge assumptions rather than simply agreeing.
